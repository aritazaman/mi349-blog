Day in My Life

This is what my typical Monday looks like during the Fall Semester of 2025 at Michigan State University, as a Computer Science major.

Published by Arita Zaman on September 23, 2025

  1. I wake up at 7:25 AM and get ready for the day.
  2. At 7:45 AM, I leave my dorm and start walking towards the STEM Building
  3. I get breakfast at the STEM Workshop.
    • I get water, a peach yogurt, and a blueberry bagel with cream cheese.
  4. I head into the lecture hall at 8:30 for CSE 380.
  5. When class ends at 9:50, I walk to Erickson Hall for my MTH 314 lecture at 10:20.
  6. When my math lecture ends at 11:40, I head towards Snyder-Phillips for lunch.
    • The lunch menu is different everyday there, but I really love SnyPhi's cheesy bread and their chocolate chip cookies.
  7. I head to my friends apartment and study there until 3PM, where I head to Bessey Hall.
  8. Until 4:20, I sit through my CSE 260 lecture.
  9. When the lecture ends, I go to the STEM Building to get some more work done, until AI Club @ 6pm.
  10. I attend the AI Club meeting until 7pm, and once that ends I've had a long day and head back to my dorm.
  11. I wrap up my studying for the day, and wind down with a movie or TV show.

Blueberry Muffin Recipe

This is my favorite recipe for making blueberry muffins.

Published by Arita Zaman on September 28, 2025

To make this recipe, you will need:

Ingredient Quantity
Melted Butter 1/2 cup
Granulated Sugar 1 cup
Large Eggs 2
Sour Cream 1/3 cup
Milk 1/3 cup
All-purpose Flour 2 cups
Baking Powder 1 and 1/2 teaspoon
Vanilla Extract 1/2 tablespoon
Salt 1/4 teaspoon
Frozen Blueberries 1 cup

Now that you have your ingredients, here's how you can turn them into delicious muffins:

  1. Pre-heat your oven to 400 degrees F.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, mix together the butter and sugar for 2 minutes.
  3. Once fully combined, add in 1 egg at a time and mix again.
  4. Then add vanilla, sour cream, and milk. Mix again and set aside.
  5. In a smaller bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, and salt.
  6. Gently fold the dry batter into the wet batter.
  7. Gently fold in the frozen blueberries.
  8. Scoop into a lined muffin pan.
  9. Bake for 25 to 30 minutes.
